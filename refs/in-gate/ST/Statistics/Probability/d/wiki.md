The Semitic letter DÃ„Âleth may have developed from the logogram for a fish or a

The equivalent Greek_letter is Delta, ÃÂ”.
The minuscule (lower-case) form of 'd' consists of a loop and a tall vertical
stroke. It developed by gradual variations on the majuscule (capital) form. In
handwriting, it was common to start the arc to the left of the vertical stroke,
resulting in a serif at the top of the arc. This serif was extended while the
rest of the letter was reduced, resulting in an angled stroke and loop. The
angled stroke slowly developed into a vertical stroke.
